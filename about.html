Who I Am

I'm Ishan Choudhury — a game developer, programmer, and creative based in the UK. I studied at the University of Greenwich and spent three years obsessively shipping games, tools, and prototypes, landing nominations at the Greenwich Digital Shark Expo three times across different projects.

My love for games started early, with a deep affection for the classics of the early 2000s — the era that shaped my design sensibilities. I'm fascinated by what makes games feel right: the interplay of mechanics, audio, and moment-to-moment feedback that turns code into experience.

What I Do

I work across the full development stack — from core gameplay programming in C# and C++, to 3D modelling environments and characters, to composing sound effects and music in FL Studio. I like owning problems end-to-end.

My main engines are Unity and Unreal Engine 5. I'm comfortable deep in UE5's back-end — I built a procedural terrain generation plugin from scratch in C++ — and equally at home rapidly iterating on Unity prototypes.

My Approach

I believe the best games are built on tight feedback loops — rapid prototyping, playtesting early, and iterating until something feels undeniably right. Whether I'm working solo or in a team, I push to have something playable as fast as possible and refine from there.

I care equally about the technical and the creative. A beautiful mechanic poorly communicated to the player is a failed mechanic. I spend as much time on juice, sound, and feel as I do on underlying systems.

Inspirations

My taste runs wide — from the cozy warmth of management sims and the tight loops of roguelikes, to the unsettling tension of psychological thrillers. Some games that shaped how I think about design:

  • Hades — masterclass in narrative-through-repetition
  • Papers Please — friction as storytelling
  • Stardew Valley — feel and tactility in every action
  • Doki Doki Literature Club — subverting player expectations
  • Katamari Damacy — the power of a truly singular idea

Let's Work Together

I'm open to opportunities in game development — as a programmer, designer, or generalist — as well as collaborations on indie projects. If something I've made resonated with you, reach out.
